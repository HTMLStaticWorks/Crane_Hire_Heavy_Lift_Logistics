Case Study

Execution of the Apex Bridge 500T Tandem Lift

Marcus Thorne
June 15, 2026 8 min read

When the state Department of Transportation mandated the replacement of the central span of the Apex Interstate Bridge, the primary constraint was time. The highway could only be fully closed for a strict 12-hour window on a Saturday night.

The Engineering Challenge

The new steel arch span weighed exactly 485 metric tons. Lifting this monolithic structure required absolute precision, perfect weather conditions, and a tandem lift operation utilizing our two largest crawler cranes.

Our engineering team spent four months preparing for this 12-hour window. Using advanced 3D CAD modeling, we simulated every phase of the lift, calculating ground bearing pressures down to the square inch to ensure the temporary matting could support the combined weight of the cranes and the load.

"In heavy lift logistics, you don't rise to the occasion; you fall to your level of preparation. For the Apex Bridge, our preparation was microscopic."
— Marcus Thorne, Chief Lift Engineer

Execution Night

At exactly 9:00 PM, the highway was closed. By 10:30 PM, the specialized SPMTs (Self-Propelled Modular Transporters) had maneuvered the new span into the staging area.

The tandem lift utilized two 600T class crawler cranes. Operating in perfect synchronization, the operators communicated via dedicated radio channels, following the exact step-by-step lift director protocol. The load was transferred from the SPMTs, hoisted 40 meters into the air, swung 45 degrees, and lowered onto the new abutments.
